# SMILE.gba - Personal Investigation Log

> **WARNING:** This repository contains backup files and visual evidence recovered from a corrupted Game Boy Advance cartridge found on October 14th. Do not attempt to replicate.

---

### Evidence Log #1: `cartridge_found.png`
*Author's note: I took this photo with my phone right before everything went wrong. That's my desk, my old console, and my hand holding that weird yellow-faced cartridge I found.*

![Evidence 1](cartridge_found.png)

It started when I found my old childhood **Game Boy Advance** buried in the back of my closet. But it wasn't alone. Lying right next to it was a grey, bootleg cartridge with no official label. Instead, someone had crudely drawn a bright yellow smiley face on it with a black marker. The smile looked twisted, almost unhinged, but I didn't think much of it at the time.

I wiped the dust off the console, placed it on my desk, and held the cartridge in my hand. Out of pure nostalgia, I snapped a quick photo. Then, I pushed the cartridge into the slot until it clicked, and flipped the power switch.

### Evidence Log #2: `ask_me_screen.png`
*Author's note: The moment I turned it on. The screen turned blood-red and that text appeared. My hand just froze.*

![Evidence 2](ask_me_screen.png)

The screen flickered, and the classic Nintendo logo instantly glitched into a pitch-black screen. But then, the display flashed with a violent, blood-red light. In the dead center of the screen, that drawn smile appeared again, but it looked different now—darker, wider, staring back at me. Suddenly, red pixelated letters began to bleed right underneath the screen, spelling out: **"A S K  M E"**. My hand froze completely over the buttons. I was too terrified to even press Start, but my phone slipped and took another shot.

Eventually, the game forced its way forward, dropping me into a silent, colorless version of *Pokémon Red*. My sprite was missing its eyes. I couldn't open the menu or use the start button. The only way to go was into a pitch-black cave. The only sound was a low, screeching audio frequency that made my temples throb.

As my eyeless sprite walked through the darkness, I encountered a wild Pokémon. The text box popped up and displayed: **"A̷̦̬͈̓̈́͜S̸̙͉̘̗̐̀́̚͠K̷̢̼̞̘̋̄̓ ̸̧͉̣̩͈̑̽̀̚͘Ḿ̸̡̢̨͉̏́̕E̸̘̼͖͙͛͒͛"**.

I selected the "FIGHT" option. Instantly, the game froze. The console speakers let out a demonic, metallic shriek that nearly deafened me. The sprite on the screen mutated—its pixelated face stretched and contorted into an impossibly wide, hollow smile. It looked exactly like the drawing on the cartridge, but alive.

I slammed the console shut in terror. I could still hear the faint static and screeching coming from the closed device, even with the power switch turned firmly to "OFF". Unable to stand the noise, I threw the Game Boy into the dumpster outside. I didn't sleep that night.

---

### Evidence Log #3: `thank_you_door.png`
*Author's note: I woke up, walked into the hallway, and saw this on my front door. It's not in the game anymore. Oh god, it's inside my house.*

![Evidence 3](thank_you_door.png)

The next morning, I woke up shaking. I walked out into the hallway to leave for work, completely exhausted. But as I reached for the doorknob, I froze. 

My mind went completely blank. All I could think was a single, terrified: *WHAT?!*

There, pressed hard against the glass window of my front door, was a bloody, bright red hand with spider-like legs creeping out from underneath it. Right above it, written in clean, horrifying letters, were two words: **"THANK YOU"**. 

It didn't want the Game Boy. It just wanted me to let it out. And now, I can hear scratching on the other side of the wood...
